## 1.0 PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the requirements, responsibilities, and structured methodology for conducting a Site Initiation Visit (SIV) at investigative sites participating in clinical trials sponsored by or contracted to the organization. The SIV is a critical regulatory milestone and represents the formal start of the operational phase of a clinical trial at an individual investigative site. The primary purpose of the SIV is to ensure that all site personnel are adequately trained on the protocol, all regulatory and essential documents are in place, investigational medicinal product (IMP) handling procedures are understood, and that the site possesses the operational readiness required to conduct the trial in compliance with applicable regulations and Good Clinical Practice (GCP) standards.

The SIV also serves as the final verification checkpoint that all site initiation activities identified during the Site Selection Visit (SSV) and Pre-Study Site Assessment have been successfully completed. Findings, training records, and confirmations generated during the SIV constitute essential regulatory documents that must be archived in the Trial Master File (TMF) and, where applicable, the Investigator Site File (ISF).

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all Clinical Research Associates (CRAs), Senior CRAs, Clinical Trial Managers, and any other sponsor or Contract Research Organization (CRO) personnel responsible for planning, conducting, or following up after an SIV. The procedure applies to all interventional Phase I–IV clinical trials sponsored by the organization, regardless of therapeutic area, study phase, or geographic region, unless a region-specific addendum specifies otherwise. This SOP also applies to investigator-initiated trials (IITs) where the organization holds regulatory sponsor responsibilities.

This SOP does not cover remote site initiation procedures, which are addressed separately under SOP-GCP-011 (Remote Site Management and Virtual Visits). Compassionate use programs and expanded access protocols are also excluded from the scope of this document.

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

This SOP has been developed in compliance with the following regulatory frameworks:

- ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice Guideline (EMA/CHMP/ICH/135/1995), specifically Sections 1.38, 4.1, 4.2, 5.18, and 8.0 (Essential Documents)
- FDA 21 CFR Part 312 — Investigational New Drug Application
- FDA 21 CFR Part 50 — Protection of Human Subjects
- FDA 21 CFR Part 56 — Institutional Review Boards
- EMA Directive 2001/20/EC and the Clinical Trials Regulation EU No 536/2014
- FDA Guidance for Industry: "Oversight of Clinical Investigations — A Risk-Based Approach to Monitoring" (August 2013)

## 2.0 DEFINITIONS AND ABBREVIATIONS

### 2.1 Definitions

The following terms are used throughout this document and carry the specific meanings defined below in the context of GCP clinical trial operations:

**Site Initiation Visit (SIV):** A formal on-site visit conducted by the sponsor or designated CRO prior to enrollment of the first subject at an investigative site. The SIV confirms site readiness, delivers protocol and GCP training to site staff, and verifies that all regulatory and operational prerequisites for study initiation have been met.

**Investigator:** A person responsible for the conduct of a clinical trial at a trial site. When a trial is conducted by a team, the investigator is the responsible leader of the team and may be called the principal investigator (PI). Reference: ICH E6(R2), Section 1.34.

**Delegation Log (Delegation of Authority Log):** A site-level document listing all members of the site's clinical trial team to whom the principal investigator has formally delegated specific trial-related duties, including the date of delegation, duties assigned, and qualification verification. Reference: ICH E6(R2), Section 4.1.5.

**Trial Master File (TMF):** The collection of essential documents that individually and collectively permits the evaluation of the conduct of a study and the quality of the data produced. The TMF shall be established at the beginning of the trial. Reference: ICH E6(R2), Section 8.1.

**Investigator Site File (ISF):** The site-maintained component of the TMF, containing all essential documents held at the investigative site. Also referred to as the Investigator's Binder.

**Green-Light Criteria:** A defined set of prerequisite conditions that must be confirmed as fully met before a site is authorized to enroll the first study subject. Green-light authorization is issued by the sponsor following successful completion of the SIV.

**Investigational Medicinal Product (IMP):** A pharmaceutical form of an active substance or placebo being tested or used as a reference in a clinical trial. Reference: EU Directive 2001/20/EC, Article 2(d).

**Protocol Deviation:** Any departure from the approved clinical trial protocol that is not prospectively approved by the sponsor and, where required, by the IRB/IEC.

## 3.0 ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Clinical Research Associate (CRA) / Site Monitor

The CRA assigned to the investigative site bears primary operational responsibility for planning, executing, and following up on the SIV. The CRA is responsible for ensuring that all pre-SIV checklists are completed prior to scheduling the visit, that all required training materials are current and approved, and that SIV documentation including the visit report, training logs, and TMF filings are completed within the timelines specified in this SOP. The CRA must hold a current GCP certification, have completed sponsor-specific CRA qualification training, and be formally authorized to conduct monitoring activities for the applicable protocol.

### 3.2 Senior CRA / Clinical Trial Manager (CTM)

The Senior CRA or CTM provides oversight and support to the assigned CRA. For higher-complexity sites (defined as sites with more than 50 planned subjects, sites in regions with heightened regulatory scrutiny, or sites identified as "high-risk" during site qualification), co-monitoring by the Senior CRA at the SIV is mandatory. The CTM is responsible for approving the final SIV agenda prior to distribution to the site and for reviewing and approving the SIV visit report within 5 business days of report submission by the CRA.

### 3.3 Principal Investigator (PI)

The PI is responsible for ensuring that all site personnel who will be involved in trial-related activities attend the SIV training sessions or complete documented make-up training within 5 business days of the SIV date. The PI must personally sign the Delegation Log and Protocol Signature Page during or within 2 business days following the SIV. The PI retains overall responsibility for the conduct of the trial at the site in accordance with the approved protocol, ICH E6(R2) Section 4.1, and applicable local regulations.

### 3.4 Site Coordinator / Sub-Investigator

Site coordinators and sub-investigators who have been delegated specific trial-related duties by the PI must attend all applicable SIV training sessions relevant to their delegated responsibilities. They must sign the Delegation Log and all applicable training documentation at the time of the SIV or within 2 business days if documented make-up training is arranged.

### 3.5 Clinical QA Specialist

The Clinical QA Specialist may attend the SIV as an observer, particularly for first-in-human studies, pivotal trials, or sites flagged during Risk-Based Monitoring (RBM) assessment. The QA Specialist is responsible for reviewing the SIV report for compliance adequacy and for issuing any associated quality findings or observations through the organization's CAPA system.

## 4.0 PRE-SIV PLANNING AND PREPARATION

### 4.1 Initiation of Pre-SIV Activities

Pre-SIV planning activities shall be initiated no later than 4 weeks prior to the target SIV date. The CRA shall confirm with the CTM that all site qualification and regulatory pre-requisites have been satisfied before commencing SIV preparation. These prerequisites are tracked via the Site Activation Tracker maintained in the Clinical Trial Management System (CTMS). A site shall not be scheduled for an SIV unless all Tier 1 activation milestones, as defined in Table 4.1 below, have been confirmed as complete.

**Table 4.1 — Site Activation Tier 1 Prerequisites (Required Before SIV Scheduling)**

| Prerequisite Document / Activity | Responsible Party | Minimum Lead Time Before SIV | Verification Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| IRB/IEC Approval Letter (study-specific) | PI / Site | Must be received ≥5 business days pre-SIV | Original or certified copy in ISF |
| Fully Executed Clinical Trial Agreement (CTA) / Contract | Sponsor Legal | Must be fully executed ≥3 business days pre-SIV | Signed contract in TMF |
| Executed Confidentiality Agreement (CDA) | Sponsor Legal | Prior to SSV | TMF filing confirmation |
| Financial Disclosure Form (FDA 21 CFR Part 54) | PI / Sub-I | Completed ≥3 business days pre-SIV | Signed forms in TMF |
| Regulatory Authority Notification / IND Authorization | Sponsor Regulatory | Prior to SIV at any site | IND number confirmed |
| Curriculum Vitae (CV) — PI and all Sub-Investigators | PI | Received and current (within 2 years) | CV log in ISF |
| GCP Training Certificates — PI and Sub-Is | PI | Current (within 2 years for most sponsors) | Certificates in ISF |
| Site Qualification Visit (SQV) Report — Approved | CRA / CTM | Completed prior to SIV | SQV report approved in TMF |
| Pharmacy Qualification Completed | Pharmacist / CRA | Completed ≥5 business days pre-SIV | Pharmacy qualification form in TMF |
| Laboratory Certification (e.g., CAP/CLIA or local equivalent) | Site Laboratory | Current | Lab certification in ISF |
| Normal Reference Ranges Provided by Site Laboratory | Site Laboratory | Received ≥5 business days pre-SIV | Signed ranges form in ISF |

### 4.2 SIV Scheduling and Confirmation

The CRA shall contact the site coordinator to identify a mutually agreeable SIV date that allows attendance by the PI, all sub-investigators, and key site personnel (coordinators, pharmacist, laboratory staff). The SIV should be scheduled for a minimum duration of 4 hours for simple/low-volume protocols and a minimum of 7 hours for complex protocols involving multiple treatment arms, imaging assessments, central laboratory procedures, or pharmacokinetic (PK) sampling. Once a date is confirmed, the CRA shall send a formal SIV Confirmation Letter to the site at least 10 business days prior to the scheduled visit date. The confirmation letter must include the proposed agenda, a list of required attendees, a list of documents the site must prepare for review, and logistical instructions.
