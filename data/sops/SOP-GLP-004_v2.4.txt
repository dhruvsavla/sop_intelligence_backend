## 1.0 PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes mandatory requirements for the recording, correction, management, and archiving of raw data generated during GLP-regulated nonclinical laboratory studies. It defines the acceptable methods for making entries into laboratory notebooks and electronic systems, the approved procedure for correcting errors in both paper and electronic records, and the responsibilities of all personnel involved in data generation and review.

The fundamental objective of this SOP is to ensure that all raw data is attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, and accurate (ALCOA), and to guarantee that the full history of any data point — including any corrections — is permanently preserved and traceable. Compliance with this procedure is mandatory for all study personnel.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all raw data generated in support of GLP-regulated nonclinical laboratory studies conducted at this facility, including but not limited to: analytical measurements, observations, calculations, instrument printouts, chromatograms, spectral data, and any derived data used in study reports. It applies to data recorded in paper laboratory notebooks, loose-leaf worksheets, printed instrument outputs, and electronic data systems.

This SOP governs the procedures for paper notebook error correction in Section 6.1 and electronic record correction in Section 6.2.

### 1.3 Regulatory Basis

This procedure is established in accordance with FDA 21 CFR Part 58 (Good Laboratory Practice for Nonclinical Laboratory Studies), specifically §58.130 (Conduct of a Nonclinical Laboratory Study) and §58.185 (Reporting of Study Results); OECD Principles of Good Laboratory Practice (ENV/JM/MONO(99)20), Principle 8 (Records and Reports); and ICH Q7 Section 6.6 (Laboratory Controls). Where national competent authority requirements are more stringent than the provisions of this SOP, the more stringent requirement shall prevail.

## 2.0 DEFINITIONS AND ABBREVIATIONS

### 2.1 Definitions

**Raw Data:** Any original laboratory worksheets, records, memoranda, notes, or exact copies thereof that result from original observations and activities of a study.

**Laboratory Notebook:** A bound or controlled-page paper record used to capture real-time observations, measurements, and experimental results during GLP study conduct.

**Error:** An unintentional incorrect entry made during the original recording of data.

**Correction:** A change made to a data entry after it has been initially recorded, using the approved single line-through method, to replace an erroneous value with the correct value while preserving the original entry.

**Audit Trail:** A secure, computer-generated, time-stamped electronic record that captures the date and time of operator entries and actions that create, modify, or delete electronic records.

**ALCOA:** Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate — the five fundamental data integrity principles required for GLP-compliant raw data.

**ALCOA+:** An extension of ALCOA adding the requirements that data be Complete, Consistent, Enduring, and Available.

**Contemporaneous:** Recorded at the time the observation or action occurs, not reconstructed from memory after the fact.

**Obliteration:** The complete or partial covering, erasing, or rendering illegible of an original data entry. Obliteration of raw data is strictly prohibited.

## 3.0 ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Analyst / Study Personnel

All analysts and study personnel are responsible for: recording raw data directly and contemporaneously into designated laboratory notebooks or electronic systems; applying the correct error correction method as defined in Section 6 whenever an erroneous entry is identified; never obliterating, erasing, or using correction fluid on any paper record; and immediately reporting to their supervisor any situation where they are uncertain about the correct correction method.

### 3.2 Study Director

The Study Director is responsible for ensuring that all personnel assigned to the study are trained on this SOP prior to initiating data collection; reviewing data entries and corrections during study conduct and at final report preparation; and escalating to Quality Assurance any pattern of corrections that may indicate systematic recording problems.

### 3.3 Quality Assurance Specialist

The QA Specialist is responsible for auditing compliance with this SOP during scheduled and unannounced inspections; reviewing the appropriateness of error corrections; documenting findings and issuing corrective action requests when non-compliant corrections are identified; and confirming that the correction procedure defined in Section 6 is consistently followed across all study notebooks.

### 3.4 Laboratory Supervisor / Section Head

The Laboratory Supervisor is responsible for: ensuring that all staff under their supervision are trained and competent in the error correction procedure; countersigning corrections that require supervisory review per Section 6.1.5; and escalating potential data integrity issues to the QA Specialist and Study Director without delay.

### 3.5 Records Management / Archivist

The Records Management function is responsible for receiving, indexing, and maintaining all completed laboratory notebooks and associated raw data in the GLP archive; ensuring that archived records are stored under conditions that preserve legibility and physical integrity; and controlling access to archived materials in accordance with SOP-DI-001.

## 4.0 RAW DATA RECORDING REQUIREMENTS AND PRINCIPLES

### 4.1 ALCOA+ Compliance

All raw data entries must satisfy the ALCOA+ principles at the time of recording:

- **Attributable:** Every entry must identify who made it. Paper records require the analyst's initials or signature and the date. Electronic entries are automatically attributed through secure system login.
- **Legible:** All entries must be permanently legible, written in permanent ink (blue or black) for paper records. Pencil is prohibited for raw data entries.
- **Contemporaneous:** Data must be recorded at the time the observation, measurement, or result is obtained. Reconstruction of data from memory after the fact is prohibited.
- **Original:** The first capture of a result constitutes the original record. Transcribed copies must be verified against the original and the original retained.
- **Accurate:** Entries must faithfully represent what was observed or measured, without alteration or misrepresentation.

### 4.2 Original Record Requirement

The original record, whether paper or electronic, must be retained in its entirety. No page, entry, or data point may be removed from a laboratory notebook or electronic system without documented QA authorization. When transcription to a secondary system is required, the original must be retained and the transcribed copy verified by a second analyst.

### 4.3 Accuracy and Completeness Requirements

Data entries must include all significant figures appropriate to the measurement; units of measurement; the date and time of the observation where scientifically relevant; and the identity of the instrument, reagent lot, or reference standard used where applicable.

### 4.4 Minimum Data Entry Requirements

At a minimum, each data-bearing page of a laboratory notebook shall contain: the study number or project code; the date in DD-MMM-YYYY format; the analyst's initials; and a brief description of the activity being recorded. Open spaces on a page must be crossed through with a single diagonal line and initialed before the page is turned, to prevent unauthorized additions.

## 5.0 LABORATORY NOTEBOOK MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 Notebook Issuance and Control

All laboratory notebooks used for GLP study data must be issued through the Records Management function, which assigns a unique notebook identification number and records the issuance date and recipient. Pre-numbered pages are required. Notebooks with removable pages are prohibited for primary GLP raw data recording.

### 5.2 Notebook Usage

Entries must be made on consecutive pages in chronological order. Pages must not be left blank. If a page is started and then the study is interrupted, the analyst must draw a line through the unused portion, date it, and initial it before proceeding. Notebooks must be stored in a secure location accessible only to authorized study personnel when not in active use.

### 5.3 Notebook Completion and Transfer to Archive

When a notebook is completed, the analyst must sign and date the final entry, and the Study Director must countersign to confirm review. Completed notebooks are transferred to the QA Specialist for audit review before forwarding to the GLP archive.

## 6.0 ERROR CORRECTION PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Error Correction in Paper Laboratory Notebooks and Worksheets

#### 6.1.1 General Prohibition on Obliteration

The obliteration of any original entry in a paper laboratory notebook or worksheet is strictly and unconditionally prohibited. This prohibition applies to all methods of obliteration, including but not limited to:

- Correction fluid (e.g., Wite-Out, Tipp-Ex) or correction tape
- Overwriting or writing over the original entry
- Heavy or multiple lines that render the original illegible
- Erasing, scratching out, or physically removing any portion of the entry
- Affixing labels, tape, or stickers that cover the original entry

Any notebook in which obliteration is discovered will be flagged as a potential data integrity deviation and referred immediately to the QA Specialist and Study Director for investigation.

#### 6.1.2 Required Correction Method — Single Line-Through

When an error is identified in a paper record, the analyst must correct it using the following single line-through method:

1. **Draw a single, straight line** through the entire incorrect entry, using permanent ink (blue or black). The line must be drawn so that the original entry remains clearly legible beneath it.
2. **Write the correct entry** immediately adjacent to, above, or below the crossed-out entry, in a location that makes the relationship between the correction and the original entry unambiguous.
3. **Write the date** of the correction in DD-MMM-YYYY format immediately beside or beneath the correction.
4. **Initial the correction** at the same location as the date, using the same initials recorded at notebook issuance.
5. **Provide a brief reason for the correction** in the space immediately adjacent to the correction mark. The reason must be specific enough to be meaningful (e.g., "transposition error", "wrong unit — should be mL not µL", "instrument misread — rechecked and corrected"). The reason "error" or "mistake" alone is not acceptable.

#### 6.1.3 Timing of Corrections

Corrections must be made at the time the error is identified. If an error is identified during a later review (e.g., by the Study Director or QA during audit), the correction must still follow the single line-through method and must include the actual date on which the correction is made — not the original date of the erroneous entry. Back-dating of corrections is a data integrity violation and is prohibited.

#### 6.1.4 Witness Countersignature Requirements

A second-person countersignature is required for corrections in the following circumstances:

- Any correction to a critical data point (e.g., a primary endpoint measurement, a dose level, a sample weight used in a stoichiometric calculation)
- Any correction made more than 24 hours after the original entry
- Any correction that changes the interpretation of a result (e.g., from within-specification to out-of-specification)
- Any correction identified during a QA audit rather than self-identified by the analyst

The countersigning individual must be a second qualified analyst, the Laboratory Supervisor, or the Study Director. The countersignature must include the countersigner's initials and the date of countersigning.

#### 6.1.5 Corrections Involving Large Volumes of Data

If a systematic error affects multiple entries across several pages (e.g., a unit conversion error or an instrument calibration offset applied to a series of measurements), the analyst must not attempt to correct each entry individually before consulting the Study Director. The Study Director and QA Specialist must jointly assess the scope and approve a documented correction plan before any changes are made. The correction plan must be appended to the notebook as a controlled amendment.

#### 6.1.6 Prohibited Actions

The following actions constitute data integrity violations and must be reported immediately to the QA Specialist:

- Making corrections on behalf of another analyst without their knowledge and countersignature
- Making corrections at the direction of a supervisor without independent scientific justification
- Destroying, removing, or altering any page of a laboratory notebook
- Reconstructing data from memory and presenting it as original contemporaneous records
- Using any form of obliteration as described in Section 6.1.1

### 6.2 Error Correction in Electronic Records

#### 6.2.1 Audit Trail Requirement

All electronic data systems used for GLP raw data recording must be configured to maintain a complete, unalterable audit trail. The audit trail must capture: the original entry; the date and time of the original entry; the analyst's electronic identity (user ID); the corrected value; the date and time of the correction; the identity of the person making the correction; and the reason for the correction entered as free text.

#### 6.2.2 Electronic Correction Method

Corrections to electronic records must be made through the system's standard edit or amendment function, which must trigger the audit trail capture described in Section 6.2.1. Direct modification of database fields, back-end edits, or any manipulation of electronic records that bypasses the audit trail is strictly prohibited and constitutes a critical data integrity violation.

#### 6.2.3 Reason for Change — Electronic Records

A free-text reason for change must be entered at the time of every electronic correction. The reason must be specific and scientifically meaningful, consistent with the requirements for paper records described in Section 6.1.2. Generic entries such as "error" or "correction" without further specification are not acceptable.

## 7.0 ELECTRONIC RECORDS AND AUDIT TRAILS

### 7.1 System Validation Requirements

All computerized systems used to capture, process, or report GLP raw data must be validated in accordance with applicable regulatory requirements before use. Validation records must be maintained in the QA archive.

### 7.2 Access Controls

Access to electronic data systems must be controlled through individual user accounts with unique passwords. Shared or generic logins are prohibited. Access rights must be assigned based on job function and must be reviewed at least annually by the system administrator and QA.

### 7.3 Backup and Recovery

Electronic raw data must be backed up at minimum daily, with backup media stored at a geographically separate location. Backup integrity must be verified quarterly. Recovery procedures must be documented and tested annually.

---

## 8.0 DATA REVIEW AND VERIFICATION

### 8.1 Second-Person Verification

All critical raw data transcriptions and calculations must be verified by a second qualified analyst before use in study reports. The verifier must sign and date adjacent to the verified entry.

### 8.2 Study Director Data Review

The Study Director must review all raw data notebooks at study completion before signing the final report. The review must confirm that all entries are legible, all corrections are compliant with Section 6, and no unapproved changes or obliterations are present.

### 8.3 QA Data Audit

The QA Specialist conducts independent data audits at intervals specified in the study plan. Findings are documented and communicated to the Study Director. Critical findings require corrective action before study report finalization.

---

## 9.0 ARCHIVING AND RETENTION

### 9.1 Retention Periods

Raw data, including laboratory notebooks, worksheets, instrument printouts, and electronic records, must be retained for a minimum of 10 years from the date of study completion, or for the period required by applicable regulations and the Sponsor's contractual requirements, whichever is longer.

### 9.2 Archive Conditions

Archived records must be stored under conditions that protect against deterioration, damage, and unauthorized access. Temperature and humidity must be monitored and documented. Paper records must be stored in acid-free containers. Electronic records must be maintained in a validated electronic archive system with access controls and audit trails.

### 9.3 Archive Access

Access to archived records is restricted to authorized personnel. All access events must be logged, recording the accessor's identity, the records accessed, the date and time, and the stated purpose of access.

---

## 10.0 DEVIATIONS AND NON-CONFORMANCES

Any deviation from the requirements of this SOP — including incorrect correction methods, obliteration of entries, unauthorized access, or failure to maintain the audit trail — must be documented in a Deviation Report within 24 hours of discovery. The Deviation Report must be reviewed and approved by the QA Specialist and Study Director. Corrective and preventive actions must be implemented and effectiveness verified. Deviations that may affect the integrity of study data must be assessed for their potential impact on study conclusions and, where necessary, disclosed in the study report.
