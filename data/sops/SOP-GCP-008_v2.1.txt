## 1.0 PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the requirements, responsibilities, and step-by-step processes for submitting clinical trial documents to Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) and Independent Ethics Committees (IECs), obtaining and maintaining required approvals, and managing all subsequent communications with those oversight bodies throughout the lifecycle of a clinical study. The procedures described herein are designed to ensure that all human subjects research conducted under the sponsorship or oversight of this organization complies fully with applicable regulations, including but not limited to FDA 21 CFR Parts 50, 56, and 312; ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice; and EMA Regulation (EU) No 536/2014. Adherence to this SOP protects the rights, safety, and well-being of trial participants and preserves the integrity of data collected in support of regulatory submissions.

This SOP also defines the documentation standards for maintaining a complete, accurate, and inspection-ready IRB/IEC communication file as part of the Trial Master File (TMF). Failure to maintain adequate IRB/IEC records constitutes a significant regulatory risk, may render clinical data unusable for regulatory submissions, and may expose the organization to enforcement actions. Accordingly, compliance with this SOP is mandatory for all personnel engaged in the conduct, oversight, or management of clinical trials.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all interventional clinical trials conducted under an Investigational New Drug (IND) application or equivalent regulatory authorization in which the organization acts as Sponsor, Sponsor-Investigator, or Contract Research Organization (CRO) with delegated IRB/IEC management responsibilities. It covers all phases (Phase I through Phase IV) and all therapeutic areas. This SOP applies to studies conducted at sites located within the United States, the European Union, and any other jurisdiction where a comparable independent ethics oversight body is required by local law or regulation.

This SOP does not govern the submission of Marketing Authorization Applications (MAAs), New Drug Applications (NDAs), or Biologics License Applications (BLAs), nor does it cover non-interventional or observational research unless such research is subject to IRB/IEC review under applicable institutional policy. Related processes for Informed Consent Form (ICF) development and management are governed by SOP-GCP-005.

## 2.0 DEFINITIONS AND ABBREVIATIONS

### 2.1 Definitions

**Amendment:** A formal change to any previously approved trial document (e.g., protocol, ICF, Investigator's Brochure) that must be submitted to and approved by the relevant IRB/IEC before implementation, unless the change is necessary to eliminate an immediate hazard to participants.

**Approval Date:** The date on which the IRB/IEC formally grants written approval for a clinical trial document or amendment, which constitutes the earliest date on which activities covered by that approval may lawfully commence.

**Continuing Review:** A periodic re-evaluation of an ongoing clinical trial conducted by the IRB/IEC at intervals not exceeding 12 months, as required by 21 CFR 56.109(f) and ICH E6(R2) Section 3.1.3, to confirm that the benefit-risk balance remains acceptable and that the study continues to comply with applicable requirements.

**Ethics Committee (EC):** An independent body constituted and operating according to local laws and regulations, responsible for ensuring the protection of the rights, safety, and well-being of human subjects involved in a trial. Synonymous with IEC in the context of this SOP.

**Expedited Review:** A review procedure used for research that (a) involves no more than minimal risk and (b) appears on the list of categories of research eligible for expedited review published by the FDA and HHS (most recently revised November 9, 1998), allowing review by the IRB chairperson or a designated experienced IRB member rather than convened full board review.

**Full Board Review:** Review of proposed research by the convened IRB/IEC at a scheduled meeting at which a quorum (majority of members) is present, required for research involving greater than minimal risk.

**IEC (Independent Ethics Committee):** A body that operates independently of the clinical trial site and the sponsor, typically used outside the United States, constituted to review and approve the conduct of a proposed clinical trial, monitor ongoing trials, and ensure participant protection.

**IRB (Institutional Review Board):** A review board established in accordance with 21 CFR Part 56 and registered with the U.S. Office for Human Research Protections (OHRP), empowered to approve, require modifications in, or disapprove research involving human subjects.

**Unanticipated Problem (UAP):** Any incident, experience, or outcome that meets all three of the following criteria: (1) unexpected in terms of nature, severity, or frequency given the research procedures and the expected outcomes; (2) related or possibly related to participation in the research; and (3) suggests that the research places subjects or others at greater risk of harm than was previously known or recognized.

## 3.0 ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Regulatory Affairs / Clinical Operations Lead

The Regulatory Affairs or Clinical Operations Lead is the primary owner of the IRB/IEC submission process and bears overall accountability for ensuring that all submissions are accurate, complete, and made within required timeframes. This individual serves as the central coordinator between the Principal Investigator (PI), the IRB/IEC, and the internal study team. The Regulatory Affairs Lead must maintain a current Submissions Tracker (see Section 4.5) and escalate overdue approvals or unresolved queries to senior management within 5 business days of the missed deadline. This role is responsible for conducting a pre-submission completeness check of all documents prior to formal submission to the IRB/IEC.

### 3.2 Principal Investigator (PI)

The PI is legally and ethically responsible for the conduct of the clinical trial at the investigative site, including compliance with all IRB/IEC directives. The PI must review and sign all documents submitted to the IRB/IEC, promptly report to the IRB/IEC any adverse events, protocol deviations, or UAPs in accordance with the timelines specified in Section 7.0, and ensure that no study procedures are initiated prior to written IRB/IEC approval. The PI may delegate specific submission tasks to a qualified Sub-Investigator or Clinical Research Coordinator, provided that delegation is documented in the Site Delegation Log.

### 3.3 Clinical Research Associate (CRA) / Monitor

The CRA is responsible for verifying at each monitoring visit that the site holds current and valid IRB/IEC approval, that the approved versions of all study documents (protocol, ICF, questionnaires) are in use, and that approval expiration dates have not elapsed. The CRA must document IRB/IEC approval status in each monitoring visit report and escalate any discrepancies to the Clinical Trial Manager (CTM) within 2 business days of discovery. During remote or centralized monitoring, the CRA verifies IRB/IEC approval currency through review of documents uploaded to the electronic TMF.

### 3.4 Quality Assurance (QA) Specialist

The QA Specialist performs periodic audits of IRB/IEC submission files to verify compliance with this SOP. The QA Specialist is responsible for reviewing the Submissions Tracker quarterly, issuing Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) requests for identified deficiencies, and providing final review of this SOP at each scheduled review cycle. The QA Specialist does not independently submit documents to the IRB/IEC but provides a pre-submission quality check upon request of the Regulatory Affairs Lead for high-risk amendments or initial submissions.

## 4.0 INITIAL IRB/IEC SUBMISSION AND APPROVAL

### 4.1 Overview of Initial Submission Requirements

Prior to the enrollment of any study participant, the Sponsor and/or PI must obtain written approval from a properly constituted IRB/IEC as required by 21 CFR 56.103 and ICH E6(R2) Section 4.4.1. The initial submission package serves as the foundational ethical and scientific review of the proposed clinical trial and must be sufficiently complete to allow the IRB/IEC to perform a thorough benefit-risk assessment. No investigational procedures, screening activities that exceed standard of care, or informed consent discussions may occur prior to receipt of written IRB/IEC approval, with the sole exception of activities necessary to screen for eligibility that present no more than minimal risk and are explicitly permitted by the specific IRB/IEC in their written procedures.

The Regulatory Affairs Lead must initiate the assembly of the submission package no later than 45 calendar days before the planned first patient first visit (FPFV) date to allow adequate time for document preparation, internal review, submission, and anticipated IRB/IEC review cycles. For studies involving vulnerable populations (pediatric participants, prisoners, pregnant women, decisionally impaired individuals), an additional 15 calendar days must be added to the preparation timeline in recognition of the additional regulatory scrutiny these categories attract under 21 CFR Part 50, Subpart D and 45 CFR Part 46, Subparts B–D.

### 4.2 Required Documents for Initial Submission

The following table specifies the documents required for a complete initial IRB/IEC submission package. All documents must reflect the version number and date in the header or footer and must be consistent with each other (i.e., the protocol version referenced in the ICF must match the protocol submitted).

| Document                                       | Minimum Content Requirements                                                    | Responsible Preparer         |
|-----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Protocol (current version)                     | Objectives, design, eligibility criteria, procedures, statistical analysis plan | Medical Director / Study Team |
| Informed Consent Form (ICF)                    | 8 required elements per 21 CFR 50.25; written at ≤8th-grade reading level       | Regulatory Affairs / PI      |
| Investigator's Brochure (IB) or SmPC           | Current edition, dated within 12 months                                         | Regulatory / Medical Affairs |
| IRB/IEC Application Form                       | IRB-specific; include IND number if applicable                                  | Regulatory Affairs Lead      |
| Investigator CV and Medical License            | Updated within 2 years; licensure current and in jurisdiction                   | PI / Sub-Investigators       |
| Site Facilities Description                    | Space, equipment, staffing relevant to protocol requirements                    | Site Coordinator             |
| Sponsor Authorization Letter                   | CRO delegation or Sponsor confirmation of trial oversight                       | Regulatory Affairs Lead      |
| Recruitment Materials                          | All advertisements, flyers, scripts; HIPAA-compliant where applicable           | Study Team                   |
| Sample Collection/Laboratory Manual            | Procedures, normal ranges, handling, shipment instructions                      | Central Laboratory           |
| Financial Disclosure Forms (where required)    | Per 21 CFR Part 54; all investigators                                           | PI / Finance                 |
