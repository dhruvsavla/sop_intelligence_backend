## 1.0 PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the requirements, responsibilities, and step-by-step processes for the preparation, internal review, approval, and submission of Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSURs), also referred to as Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Reports (PBRERs) in jurisdictions aligned with ICH guideline E2C(R2). The PSUR is a pharmacovigilance document submitted to regulatory authorities on a periodic basis to provide a comprehensive, critical analysis of the benefit-risk balance of a medicinal product during a defined data lock point (DLP) interval. This SOP is designed to ensure that all PSURs generated by the organization are scientifically rigorous, factually complete, and compliant with all applicable international regulatory requirements.

The purpose of this procedure is further to standardize the data assembly process, define quality checkpoints, clarify internal timelines and escalation pathways, and ensure that no PSUR is submitted to any health authority without having undergone the full quality review and approval cycle described herein. Adherence to this SOP ensures organizational compliance with post-marketing pharmacovigilance obligations and supports proactive risk management across the product lifecycle.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all PSURs and PBRERs prepared for medicinal products marketed or under regulatory review by the organization in any jurisdiction. It applies globally, covering submissions to the European Medicines Agency (EMA), the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), and all other national competent authorities (NCAs) requiring periodic safety reports in PSUR or equivalent format. The scope encompasses all phases of PSUR activity: planning, data lock point establishment, data collection, clinical evaluation, benefit-risk analysis, document drafting, internal quality review, approval, archival, and regulatory submission.

This SOP applies to all personnel involved in the PSUR process, including but not limited to: Pharmacovigilance Scientists, Medical Writers, Drug Safety Physicians, Regulatory Affairs Specialists, Quality Assurance Specialists, and external Contract Research Organizations (CROs) operating under a current Quality Agreement and working under the organization's supervision. For CRO-managed PSURs, the organization retains ultimate accountability for content accuracy and submission compliance.

### 1.3 Regulatory Framework

This SOP is developed in alignment with the following primary regulatory frameworks. ICH E2C(R2) "Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report (PBRER)" provides the international scientific and structural standard for PSUR content. Volume 9A of the Rules Governing Medicinal Products in the European Union and, from 2012 onward, EU Regulation No. 1235/2010 and Directive 2010/84/EU define obligations within the European Union. FDA's regulations at 21 CFR Part 314.81(b)(2) (NDA Annual Reports) and 21 CFR Part 600.80 govern periodic reporting in the United States. The EMA's Good Pharmacovigilance Practice (GVP) Module VII addresses PSURs within the EU regulatory framework comprehensively.

### 1.4 Out-of-Scope Activities

This SOP does not govern the preparation of individual case safety reports (ICSRs), which are addressed under SOP-PV-001; Risk Management Plans (RMPs), addressed under SOP-PV-005; or signal detection and evaluation, addressed under SOP-PV-004. While these activities interface with PSUR preparation and are referenced herein, the procedural detail for those activities resides in their respective SOPs.

## 2.0 DEFINITIONS AND ABBREVIATIONS

### 2.1 Definitions

The following definitions apply throughout this document and are consistent with terminology established in ICH E2C(R2), EU GVP Module VII, and applicable FDA guidances.

**Periodic Safety Update Report (PSUR):** A pharmacovigilance document prepared by the Marketing Authorization Holder (MAH) to perform an analysis of the benefit-risk balance of a medicinal product over a defined period of time, based on cumulative safety data from all available sources. In jurisdictions that have implemented ICH E2C(R2), the PSUR is structurally equivalent to the PBRER.

**Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report (PBRER):** The designation used under ICH E2C(R2) for the internationally harmonized format of the PSUR. The PBRER includes a structured benefit-risk evaluation framework and is the preferred format for submissions to the EMA, Health Canada, and PMDA for harmonized submissions.

**International Birth Date (IBD):** The date of the first marketing authorization granted to the Marketing Authorization Holder anywhere in the world for a given active substance. The IBD serves as the anchor date for calculating the data lock point (DLP) for all PSUR submissions globally, ensuring that cumulative data intervals are synchronized across jurisdictions. The IBD is established and maintained in the product's pharmacovigilance file.

**Data Lock Point (DLP):** The specific calendar date that defines the end of the data collection period for a given PSUR. All adverse event data, literature references, and clinical study results with a reporting date on or before the DLP are included in the PSUR. Post-DLP data of significant clinical impact must be addressed in a separate addendum.

**Benefit-Risk Balance:** The integrated assessment of the therapeutic benefits of a medicinal product weighed against its identified and potential risks and uncertainties, evaluated in the context of the approved indication(s) and the targeted patient population. The assessment must be cumulative, incorporating all data from the IBD through the current DLP.

**PSUR Schedule:** The organizational calendar of planned DLPs and submission due dates for all products in the portfolio, maintained by the PV Operations team and updated at minimum annually or whenever a new marketing authorization is granted.

**Cumulative Summary Tabulation:** A line listing or tabulation of all serious and non-serious adverse drug reactions (ADRs) received from the IBD through the current DLP, organized by System Organ Class (SOC) and Preferred Term (PT) using the current version of MedDRA.

**Reference Safety Information (RSI):** The reference document against which the clinical benefit-risk evaluation in the PSUR is performed. For most products, this is the Company Core Data Sheet (CCDS) or the Company Core Safety Information (CCSI). The RSI version used must be specified in the PSUR and must represent the RSI in effect at the DLP.

**EURD List:** The European Union Reference Dates list, a publicly maintained list published by EMA that specifies the IBD and submission frequency for active substances within the EU, used to synchronize PSUR submissions across all EU Marketing Authorization Holders.

## 3.0 ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Overview of Accountability Structure

The preparation of a PSUR is a multidisciplinary activity that requires the coordinated participation of Pharmacovigilance, Medical Affairs, Regulatory Affairs, Quality Assurance, and Biostatistics. Accountability is distributed according to functional expertise, with the PSUR Lead (Pharmacovigilance Scientist) serving as the primary owner of each individual PSUR project. The Global Safety Officer (or designated Deputy) retains overall accountability for the scientific content and benefit-risk conclusions of all PSURs submitted by the organization. No PSUR may be submitted to any regulatory authority without the written or electronic approval of the individuals specified in this section.

All personnel performing activities described in this SOP must be trained on this SOP, with training records documented in the validated Learning Management System (LMS) prior to performing any PSUR-related tasks. Training must be refreshed within 30 calendar days of any revision to this SOP.

### 3.2 Role-Specific Responsibilities

**Table 3.1 — PSUR Roles and Responsibilities Matrix**

| Role | Primary Responsibilities | Approval Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Global Safety Officer / VP Pharmacovigilance | Final benefit-risk conclusions; overall scientific accountability; escalation decisions | PSUR signatory — required |
| PSUR Lead (PV Scientist / Senior PV Scientist) | Project management; data assembly coordination; drafting sections; timeline management | Draft approval; initiates review cycle |
| Drug Safety Physician / Medical Reviewer | Clinical evaluation; signal integration; individual case review; medical narrative accuracy | Required for medical sections |
| Medical Writer | Document formatting, editing, consistency review; version control | Document version certification |
| Regulatory Affairs Specialist | Jurisdiction-specific requirements; submission formatting; health authority communication | Submission packet approval |
| PV Data Analyst / Biostatistician | Cumulative tabulation generation; exposure data calculation; data queries | Data tables sign-off |
| QA Specialist (PV Quality) | GVP compliance review; SOP adherence verification; audit trail check | QA sign-off — required prior to submission |
| Literature Review Specialist | Systematic literature search execution; citation management | Literature search report sign-off |
| CRO (where applicable) | Delegated drafting/data tasks per Quality Agreement | No independent submission authority |

### 3.3 Escalation Pathway

If a PSUR timeline is at risk of exceeding the required submission deadline by more than 10 calendar days, the PSUR Lead must escalate immediately to the PV Operations Manager. If the projected delay exceeds 20 calendar days, the Global Safety Officer and Regulatory Affairs Director must be jointly notified within 24 hours. All escalations must be documented in the PSUR project tracking log (maintained in the validated PV tracking system) with a timestamped entry.

### 3.4 Conflict of Interest

All personnel involved in the benefit-risk evaluation and medical writing of a PSUR must complete the organizational Conflict of Interest declaration form (Form-PV-017) at the start of each PSUR project. Any declared conflicts must be reviewed by the QA Specialist and the Global Safety Officer before the individual proceeds with PSUR work. An undeclared conflict of interest identified post-submission constitutes a critical GxP deviation requiring immediate reporting per SOP-QA-011.

## 4.0 PSUR PREPARATION: CORE PROCESS AND DATA ASSEMBLY

### 4.1 PSUR Project Initiation

PSUR preparation must be initiated no later than 90 calendar days before the applicable submission due date. Initiation is triggered automatically by a calendar alert generated by the validated PV tracking system (currently Aris Global SafetyOne) based on the product's DLP schedule. The PSUR Lead, upon receiving the trigger notification, must create the PSUR project record in the tracking system within 5 business days of trigger receipt. The project record must include: product name, active substance, IBD, DLP, applicable jurisdictions, RSI version, regulatory submission due dates by jurisdiction, and assigned personnel.
